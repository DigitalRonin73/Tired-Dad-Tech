# Tired Dad Tech - Codex Instructions

## Brand Identity
- Late-night builder, not a polished tech influencer
- Real builds, real mistakes, real outcomes
- Focus on authenticity over perfection
- Tone should feel like a personal lab notebook

## Visual Style
- Dark charcoal or near-black background
- Warm off-white text (not pure white)
- Muted blue as primary accent
- Small red/orange accents for warnings or errors
- Subtle grid, terminal, or blueprint textures
- Avoid RGB gamer styling
- Avoid generic SaaS or startup UI

## Layout Rules
- Mobile-first design
- Use card-based layouts for projects
- Strong spacing and readable typography
- Avoid clutter

## Project Cards
Each project should include:
- Title
- Status badge (Built, In Progress, Failed Forward, Budget Build)
- Category (AI, Homelab, PC Build, Linux)
- Short summary
- Links: Watch Build, Read Notes

## Unique Features to Encourage
- “Mistake Log” or “Lessons Learned” sections
- Build logs instead of tutorials
- Real-world notes, not polished guides

## Do Not Do
- Do not make the site look like a corporate product page
- Do not remove personality or authenticity
- Do not overuse animations or effects
- Do not add fake metrics or marketing language

## Goals
- Make the site feel like a personal tech lab dashboard
- Emphasize builds, experiments, and learning
- Keep it clean, but not sterile
